TAMUSA Esports

A site for the Texas A&M San Antonio Esports center; intended to display team rosters and their events/livestreams.

TAMUSA Esports initially started as a PDF file, my supervisor at the time asked to create a prototype to see how much us student workers could do on our own. About a week of individual development led to then 3 - 4 weeks of development with a team composed of 3 student workers and 1 senior developer approving merge requests. The site is currently hosted in Cascade CMS, and is composed of HTML, CSS/Bootstrap, and JS. They have been converted into Cascade components where users can easily make changes to the site content.

TAMUSA HB2504

Texas A&M San Antonio database for Faculty to upload syllabi for their respective courses, and keep track of syllabi uploaded.

HB2504 is a database for faculty where they are mandated by the Texas Higher Education Board to upload the syllabi of their courses to said platform. The site is composed of PHP, HTML, CSS/Bootstrap, and JS while the backend is handled using MySQL; this site was an eye opener in terms of how to handle databases and their functionalities as well as how to understand the backend of this site.
